Web Ministry Features

  • Social Devotional
    Social Devotional: Our mission is to educate, encourage and equip the church with every effective technology. - Educate: Clarify the nature of God and creation - Encourage: Stir the faith of the young, tired, & dry believer - Equip: Supply resources to stand against evil Our plan is to spread the existing work of apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers as wise stewards of the technology gifted to our generation. Ephesians 4:11-15 Now these are the gifts Christ gave to the church: the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, and the pastors and teachers. Their responsibility is to equip God's people to do his work and build up the church, the body of Christ. This will continue until we all come to such unity in our faith and knowledge of God's Son that we will be mature in the Lord, measuring up to the full and complete standard of Christ. Then we will no longer be immature like children. We won't we tossed and blown about by every wind of new teaching. We will not be influenced when people try to trick us with lies so clever they sound like truth. Instead we will speak the truth in love, growing in every way more and more like Christ, who is the head of his body, the church.
    • Features
      This section explains the functionality of the site. It describes each system and details how they may work together to educate, encourage and equip the church. Each feature answers the following questions: 1) Why build this feature? 2) Who will benefit? 3) What will the feature do? Although the features are organized into sections, the wireframes (mockups) provide a better understanding of the site's structure.
      • Library
        In order to educate and encourage The site contains a 'Library' of resources - organized into 'Studies' and grouped by topic.
        • Library Home
          In order to discover new and familiar resources Any user May search and scroll through the site's Studies - Studies are presented as tiles and sorted by a popularity algorithm (popularity is a function of ratings, recency, controversy, and staff weighting) - Page scrolls 'infinitely' (similar to twitter) - Search autocompletes w/ suggestions. Results appear in the same tile format - Main landing page for logged out users
          • Search Library
            In order to address specific questions about their faith A member Wants an autocomplete search field to look through all studies/lessons
          • Most Popular (Discover)
            In order to discover new resources A member Wants a page that suggests new & popular studies - use ycombinator algorithm (http://www.righto.com/2013/11/how-hacker-news-ranking-really-works.html)
        • Personal Library
          In order to discover new and familiar resources A logged-in member May search and scroll through recommended Studies and Groups - Main landing page for logged in members
          • Current Studies
            In order to keep members engaged in their studies A member Can find their 'studies in progress' in their Personal Library
          • Suggested for You
            In order to provide relevant resources A member May receive suggested studies based on their search/viewing history
          • Get Connected
            In order to create community A member May receive suggestions for other members studying similar lessons
        • Single Lesson
          In order to engage a study by listening to a single lesson Any user May download or play the lesson, and browse the conversation Logged-in members May join the discussion, and create groups around the study
          • Media Player
            In order to spread quality teaching Any user May view the lesson as audio or video (when available)
          • Audio Download
            In order to spread quality teaching Any user May download an audio copy of the lesson
          • Share a lesson
            In order to spread personally meaningful teaching Any user May share a lesson via email or social media - The system should include the required metadata to most improve a secondary viewer's experience
            • Open Graph Tags
              In order for lessons to be playable (when shared) on Facebook & Google+ The system Must present OpenGraph metadata for the lesson Suggested: - og:title= "<lesson title> from <church name>" - og:type= "video.episode" (plus video metadata) - og:image= poster image (plus image metadata) - og:url= socialdevotional.com/lesson url - og:site_name= "Social Devotional" Resources: - http://ogp.me/ - https://developers.facebook.com/tools/debug - http://www.jwplayer.com/blog/publish-your-videos-to-facebook-with-a-jw-player/
            • "Share This" widget
              In order to share a lesson with friends and acquaintances Any user May share a lesson on popular sites via a "Share This" widget - Facebook - Google+ - Pinterest - Twitter - Email Resources: - http://www.addthis.com/
          • Member Conversation
            In order to engage, educate, and equip members The system Provides tools to focus and foster discussion around spiritual study (the system will discourage ad-hoc debates; by focusing on specific teaching)
            • [Shared] Input Tools
              In order to have rich user-lead communities The system provides tools to format written text, and ways to unobtrusively reference scripture, teaching and external content.
              • Basic Formatting
                In order to enhance the readability of these notes A member May use basic formatting like: - Bold, Italic, Underline - Ordered, Unordered Lists - Indentation, Blockquotes
              • Bible Reference Lookup
                In order to enhance the scriptural foundation of a lesson When a bible verse is added The system provides a popover of the verse And a link to the verse in context - use Logos auto-ref plugin (styling required!) - http://reftagger.com
              • Auto wrap links
                In order to un-clutter user input When a member adds a url to a post The system should: - wrap the url in an anchor tag (nofollow, target=blank, title=original_url) - anchor body is truncated at 30 char Bonus Points: - Fetch the url.title and set that as the anchor body - UI allows members to: --- update anchor body --- add a hyperlink to existing text
              • Reference a section of the lesson
                In order to engage with (and discuss) the current message A member May 'highlight' a specific section (time range) of the message - This should appear as a link in the question, and should play the selected portion of the lesson
                • Select the 'highlighted' section
                  In order to engage with (and discuss) the current message A member May select a range in the media player that can be: - shared as a link - pasted (and nicely formatted) in the notes/questions
                • Play the 'highlighted' section
                  In order to engage with (and discuss) the current message A member May play the 'highlighted' section of the lesson By clicking or visiting the 'highlighted' link
            • Take Notes
              In order to engage the lesson A member May take notes as the lesson is playing
              • [Shared] Input Tools
                Includes everything in [Shared] Input Tools
              • Auto Bookmarks
                In order to enhance the review-value of these notes When a member creates a new line or list-item The system stores the current playhead position (assuming the lesson is playing) And provides an icon-link to jump to that position.
            • Ask Question
              In order to more personally engage a lesson or better understand a topic A member May post a question (to a specific lesson) for other members to answer (A leader may also use this feature to drive conversation around a study)
              • [Shared] Input Tools
                Includes everything in [Shared] Input Tools
              • Submit a question
                In order for the community to grow together A member May submit questions (about the lesson) to the group
              • Search/Confirm your question
                In order to group similar questions When a member submits a question The system presents similar questions and asks the user to: "Confirm this is a new question" - Similar questions not scoped to this lesson
              • Ask a specific group-of-people
                In order to leverage trusted interpersonal relationships A member May direct a question to: - public - friends - selected group of users
              • Edit your question
                In order to promote true understanding A member May edit their question at a later time - Answers created for a previous version will indicate that they answer a previous version, and have an expandable section that shows the question as it existed when the answer was created
              • Remove your question
                In order for members to manage their posts A member May remove their question at any time in the future - soft delete the question - answers go into a user's 'archived answers' feed
              • Notify Me w/ Answers
                The member's email MUST be verified before notifications can be sent. - The UI should store the user's preference... but -- No notifications should be sent until the email is verified -- asking for multiple notifications should trigger one verification email per day
                • Email each answer
                  In order to receive immediate feedback for a question A member May be notified by email each time the question is answered The email must include: - the question & answer - a link to the context - tokenized links to star or block the user/content - tokenized options to change the notification frequency (tokenized links allow the action w/out logging in)
                • Email friend's answers
                  In order to receive immediate feedback from friends A member May be notified by email each time a friend answers a question The email must include: - the question & answer - a link to the context - tokenized links to star or block the user/content - tokenized options to change the notification frequency (tokenized links allow the action w/out logging in)
                • Email answer digest
                  In order to limit the frequency of email notifications A member May receive a daily digest of any activity on this question - No activity... no email - If a user elects to receive a digest for multiple posts, the content should be bundled into a single digest (for all questions, comments, etc.)
                • Email friend's digest
                  In order to limit the frequency of email notifications A member May receive a daily digest of any friend activity on this post - No friend activity... no email - If a user elects to receive a digest for multiple questions, the content should be bundled into a single digest (for all questions, comments, etc.) This will respect the potentially mixed preference to receive a digest for friends or all feedback on a given post.
                • No Emails for this Question
                  This is the default and an option even after notifications are sent
            • Answer a Question
              In order to more personally engage a lesson or better understand a topic A member May post a question (to a specific lesson) for other members to answer (A leader may also use this feature to drive conversation around a study)
              • [Shared] Input Tools
                Includes everything in [Shared] Input Tools
              • Submit an answer
                In order for the community to grow together A member May submit an answer to an existing question - a member may answer their own question (usually providing a unified answer from their learning)
              • Edit your answer
                In order to clarify or improve an answer A member May edit their answer after posting - technically edits may overwrite the previous record - versions not required (contrasted to Questions, where versions are required)
              • Remove your answer
                In order for members to manage their posts A member May remove their answer at any time in the future - soft delete the post
            • Browse Questions
              In order to find answers to their own questions A member May browse through existing questions using search, popularity, recency, etc.
              • Search Questions
                In order to find answers to their own questions A member May search through existing questions using keywords Example: Searching "baptism" returns: --- "Is baptism required?" --- "Whats the point of baptism" --- "Why did Jesus need to be baptized" --- "What age is a person ready to be baptized"
              • Sort Questions
                In order to browse and understand the conversation As a member I want to sort questions by: - Most Popular - Most Recent - Timeline (chronological)
              • Share a Question
                In order to share a great question with friends and acquaintances Any user May share a public question on popular sites via a "Share This" widget - Facebook - Google+ - Pinterest - Twitter - Email Resources: - http://www.addthis.com/
              • Rate a Question
                In order to identify quality content and manage the community A member May rate a question as "Useful" - Star: simple +1 - may not rate your own content
              • Block
                In order to protect members from irrelevant or abusive users and content A member May block any person or content - removing it from their experience
                • Question
                  In order to maintain a healthy learning environment A member May 'block' any question: - Blocking a question forever removes it from their UI - Reports a concern - The offending user is NOT notified If a user has blocked N posts from a single user the system Should suggest that they block the user - Blocking a user removes this and all posts by this user, and reports a concern - The offending user is NOT notified
                • User
                  In order to protect members from potently abusive users (spammers, bullies) A member May 'block' a user: - Blocking a user removes this and all posts by this user, and reports a concern - The offending user is NOT notified
            • Browse Answers
              In order to find a compelling answer to their own questions A member May scroll through existing answers sorted by popularity, recency, or timeline
              • Sort Answers
                In order to browse and understand the conversation As a member I want to sort answers by: - Most Popular - Most Recent - Timeline (chronological)
              • Share an Answer
                In order to share a great answer with friends and acquaintances Any user May share a public answer on popular sites via a "Share This" widget (the metadata will show both the question and 'shared' answer) - Facebook - Google+ - Pinterest - Twitter - Email Resources: - http://www.addthis.com/
              • Rate an Answer
                In order to identify quality content and manage the community A member May rate an answer as "Useful" - Star: simple +1 - may not rate your own content
              • Block
                In order to protect members from irrelevant or abusive users and content A member May block any person or content - removing it from their experience
                • Answer
                  In order to maintain a healthy learning environment A member May 'block' any answer: - Blocking an answer, forever removes it from their UI - Reports a concern - The offending user is NOT notified If a user has blocked N posts from a single user The system Should suggest that they block the user - Blocking a user removes this and all posts by this user, and reports a concern - The offending user is NOT notified
                • User
                  In order to protect members from potently abusive users (spammers, bullies) A member May 'block' a user: - Blocking a user removes this and all posts by this user, and reports a concern - The offending user is NOT notified
            • Comment on an answer
              In order to promote accountability through discussion A member May comment on another user's answer - Comments should be limited to 250 char - Sorted by usefulness (user rating)

              • Rate Comments

                • Comment

This will respect the potentially mixed preference to receive a digest for friends or all feedback on a given post.

      • Groups
        In order for members to move through a study together (think 'Small Group') A collection of members May self-organize into a "Group" around a single Study Groups provide a safe space to engage a Study - questions, answers, & comments are only visible to group members - group leaders take special responsibility for questions and moderation - members may view lessons at any time, but... the group moves through the study on an 'official' schedule
        • Group Membership
          In order to share a more intimate conversation around a Study A member May join a Group of other members who together w/ a leader explore a study
          • Find
            In order to join a Group A member May browse and search through all open/public groups
            • Browse All Studies
              In order to find a Group (on some topic of interest) A member May browse and search across all currently open/public Groups (search study topics) - groups with existing 'friends' should be called-out
            • Browse Single Study
              In order to find a Group for a specific study A member May search and browse all currently open/public groups for a study - if no groups are open, the system should recommend similar groups for similar studies AND prompt the user to start a group for this study
          • Join
            In order to participate in a group study A member May 'join' any open group
          • Leave
            In order to manage Group memberships A member May 'leave' a group at any time
        • Member Dashboard
          In order to manage activity across a number of groups A member May browse the aggregated activity of their groups and find archived groups (archived groups are older groups that no longer meet)
          • My Groups
            In order to return to my Groups (past & present) A member May see a list of all Groups of which they are a part - Current Groups - Archived Groups
          • Recent Q&A
            In order to draw members into their group's conversation A member May see recently asked or answered questions in the sidebar of their portal (sidebar widget)
          • Group Activity Feed
            In order to catch up on the conversation A group member May see all group activity in a single feed. Activity includes: - New lesson (watch now) - New questions or answers - Group-wide messages from the leader
          • Group Lesson View
            In order for groups to experience a lesson together A group Will have a curated view of a Lesson Tabs: - Personal Notes - Group Q&A: Only their Q&A - Public Q&A: (public questions in another tab) - Group Feed
            • Personal Notes
              In order to engage a lesson A member May take personal notes from the group's view of the lesson
            • Group Q&A
              In order to engage a lesson as a group A group Will have a private (group-wide) discussion section (Questions, Answers and Comments)
            • Public Q&A
              In order to engage in the broader conversation around a lesson A group Will have a public discussion section for the lesson (Shows the discussion happening outside of the group)
            • Group Feed
              In order to catch-up on the group's activity A group Will have an 'Activity Feed' that aggregates the group's activity into a timeline Activity: - Start a New Lesson - Questions - Answers - Comments
        • Create / Become Leader
          In order to start a more intimate conversation around a Study A member (now 'leader') May create a Group (the organizer is the default leader, but may invite co-leaders) Groups may be in ONE of the following types: - Open: Searchable and may be joined by any member - Secret: NOT searchable and may be joined by any member with a secret url - Closed: NOT joinable or searchable by any member - Archived: Closed and finished study (according to the official schedule)
          • Edit
            In order to manage a Group A leader May update the groups details, review the membership (and remove members)
          • Leave / Delete
            In order to step down as a Group leader A leader May (soft) delete a group until another leader is found - Leader is removed and the Group is archived - Members still have access to the group (as Archived) - If no (co) leader is available, members get a notification asking them to opt-in as leader
          • Group-wide Message
            In order to communicate with the group A leader May message the entire group (same message sent to each member) - added to the group feed
          • Nominate Co-leaders
            In order to share the responsibilities of leadership A leader May invite another member(s) to become a group leader
            • Create a Leader Nomination
              In order to share the responsibilities of leadership A leader May invite another member(s) to become a group leader (with the option of a personal-message) - A nomination will trigger a message and add a note to the nominee's group page - Nominees should be listed in the leader's dashboard for review (and resend/cancel actions)
            • Accept Leader Nomination
              In order to share the responsibilities of leadership A nominated leader May accept any time before the invite is canceled or the group ends - Nominees MUST agree to the 'Leadership Responsibilities' (a few paragraphs describing the requirements and expectations of a leader) - Acceptance will notify the original nominator - On acceptance, the nominee is upgraded to Leader & redirected to the group page -- If this is the member's only leadership position, they should be 'onboarded'
            • Decline Leader Nomination
              In order to recruit only willing and engaged leaders A nominated leader May decline a leadership invitation with an optional message - Nominator is notified - The invitation is removed - The nominee is asked if they would 'still like to join the group as a member'
            • Resend Leader Nomination
              In order to manage co-leadership requests (no reply etc.) A leader May resend the invitation any time before the group ends
            • Cancel Leader Nonination
              In order to manage co-leadership requests (no reply etc.) A leader May cancel the nomination any time before the nominee accepts
          • Leadership Onboarding
            In order to cultivate quality group leaders A first-time leader Will receive a mix of guidance and resources Immediate: Onscreen Tour In-depth: Followup Email Ongoing: Leadership Library
            • Onscreen Tour
              In order to immediately orient group leaders A first-time leader Will click through a 'tour' of the leadership features of their group's page
            • Followup Email
              In order to immediately orient group leaders A first-time leader Will receive an email describing the tools and resources available to leaders Includes: - Leadership Responsibilities summary - A link to trigger the click-through tour again - Leadership Library link w/ top articles
            • Leadership Library
              In order to continually sharpen group leaders A first-time leader Will be directed to the "Leadership Library" - a single page that highlights leadership focused resources across the site
          • Invite group members
            In order to jump-start a Group's membership Current and future members May be invited to join a group
            • Existing members
              In order to jump-start a Group's membership Any group member (starting w/ the leader as the first member) May search/invite any current member - max 10 invites to prevent spam - blocked members are silently prevented from contact - autocomplete tags similar to google+ (http://tagedit.webwork-albrecht.de/playground.html)
            • Non-members
              In order to jump-start a Group's membership A leader May type/paste a series of emails and an optional personal message - 30 max (ever) - Invited emails should NEVER be emailed again (unless they join) - If recipients respond w/ 'received mistakenly', the leader goes to moderation - autocomplete tags similar to google+ (http://tagedit.webwork-albrecht.de/playground.html)
          • Remove a Group Member
            In order to protect the group from abusive members (hate, bullies, spam) A leader May remove a member from the Group w/ an optional message - removes the problem-member and blocks the member's re-entry - removes the user's content from the group - blocks future invitations to this group (as they cannot be used) - the leader should be prompted to confirm the action
            • Email Notification
              In order to prevent confusion around the removal A removed group member Will receive an email with: - a description of the situation - the leader's personal message - a link and description of the appeal process
            • Appeal Process
              In order to prevent abuse of leadership and maintain a just system A removed group member May appeal the removal - the removed member must provide an explanation of their case (< 500 words) - a staff member will review the issue and provide a ruling
              • Removal Upheld
                In order to support a reasonable 'removal' A staff member May choose from a list of carefully worded form-letters explaining the reasons for the removal - staff will also have the option to suspend the problem-member
              • Removal Reversed
                In order to reverse an unreasonable 'removal' A staff member May 'undo' a leader's removal (expecting this to be rare) - the group member is re-admitted to the group - the leader is contacted by the staff member - depending on the situation the leader may instead be removed
        • Leader Dashboard
          In order to effectively lead A group leader Has a 'Dashboard' of tools to aggregate and moderate group interactions
          • Leadership Feed
            In order to effectively engage and moderate a group's discussion A group leader Will have an enhanced Activity Feed including: - The opportunity to answer new or controversial questions inline - The opportunity to block questionable content inline
            • Q&A
              In order to quickly engage new or controversial questions The system Will spotlight new or controversial questions in a leader's Activity Feed
            • Moderation
              In order to correct or eliminate potentially abusive users/content The system Will spotlight suspicious posts in a leader's Activity Feed - content that has been blocked by 2 or more users - If marked "OK" the leader will not see this 'issue' again - If there is an ongoing reaction to the post, staff is notified (via their moderation feed). The system trusts the leadership, but provides the group with a louder voice.
          • Leadership Library
            In order to encourage and support leaders in their responsibilities A group leader May access a library of resources about the study and leadership in general
            • Study Resources
              In order to deeply engage the group's study A group leader May access study specific resources like: - Small group resources from the church - Common questions & answers (from the public discussion) - Questions and commentary provided by the church or staff
            • Leadership Resources
              In order to encourage and support leaders in their responsibilities A group leader May access a library of resources on Christian leadership - This area will be common to all group leaders. The content should include resources on discipleship, ministry development, conflict resolution, etc. - This area will be curated by a staff member
          • Study / Leader Forum
            In order for leaders to support and encourage one another A leader Has a private Q&A section for all leaders (past & present) of this Study - Leaders may opt-out of all notifications similar to other sections - Leaders may block another member similar to other sections
      • Membership
        In order to join groups, save notes, and take part in a conversation A user May become a Social Devotional 'member' by creating an account
        • Account Managment
          In order to manage their system preferences A member May access and update their settings through an 'Account Management' section
          • Update Personal Data
            In order for members to maintain an accurate online identity A member May update their personal data: - email - name - profile picture
          • Reset Password
            In order for members to more easily remember account credentials A member May change their password through their profile or by email
          • Cancel Account
            In order for a user to feel safe sharing their thoughts and information A member May cancel their account at any time - The user's account is removed (but not deleted) - Their content is removed from the site - They may 're-inflate' their account at any time via email confirmation
          • Review Posts
            In order to reference previously posted content A member May review any content they have posted and if applicable jump to that context
            • Search
              In order to find previously posted content (even after it's been archived or removed ) A member May search across all the content they have posted - unified search
            • View
              In order to reference previously posted content A member May review any content they have posted in a single chronological feed And if applicable jump to that context
          • Email Preferences
            In order to manage the frequency of all emails sent from the site A member May select their email preferences and manage specific exceptions. What are exceptions? Examples: - "In general I don't want to receive any emails, but I have opted-in to immediately receive answers to a question I asked." - "In general I receive one email digest per week, but I have opted-in to receive messages from friends in real time."
            • Default Preference
              In order to broadly express their email preferences A member May express the default frequency of emails from both Friends and Any Member Options: - Real Time - Daily Summary - Weekly Summary - Never Email Notifications
              • Real Time
                In order to get instant feedback A member May opt to receive emails as things happen - Members should confirm this option as it will cause a lot of noise Example: "A friend messages me and I receive an email right away"
              • Daily Summary
                In order to limit the frequency of email notifications to once daily A member May receive a daily digest of relevant activity and personal messages - the user should be given the option of 'morning' or 'afternoon' emails Example: "I only have time to look at the site after the kids are in bed"
              • Weekly Summary
                In order to limit the frequency of email notifications to once weekly A member May receive a weekly digest of relevant activity and personal messages - the user should be given the option of which day to receive the digest Example: "I generally check on my group and watch the lesson over the weekend" This is the default email frequency
              • Never Email Notifications
                In order to turn off all email notifications A member May opt to never receive email notifications - This can still be overridden by Opt-In Exceptions
            • Opt-In Exceptions
              Opt-in exceptions happen all over the site. Any time a user decides to receive notifications around a question, lesson, friend, etc. In order to manage the accumulation of opt-in exceptions A member May update all these preferences from their 'Account' section
          • Manage Blocked Members
            In order to unblock a previously blocked user A member May manage their blocked members in their 'Account' section
            • Search Blocked Members
              In order to find a previously blocked member (in a large list) A member May search their blocked members
            • List Blocked Members
              In order to find a previously blocked member (to unblock) A member May see a list of their blocked members
            • Unblock Member
              In order to manage blocked users (perhaps blocked by accident) A member May unblock a blocked-member at any time
        • Notifications
          In order to notify members (who may have opted out of email notifications) A member May be alerted through an on-site notification system - the menubar will display the presence and number of notifications - notifications may be viewed in the message section of a member's account
          • System Messages
            In order to notify members (who may have opted out of email notifications) A member May receive system-wide announcements (like a new feature) via the in-site messaging system
          • Subscribed Events
            In order to notify members (who may have opted out of email notifications) A member May receive event-driven notifications (like someone has answered your question) via the on-site messaging system
        • Community
          In order to engage with the community and create your own circle A member May message other members, follow friends, and (if needed) block abusive users
          • Message Member
            In order to safely communicate with other members A member May use the site's messaging service to communicate on-site or through email Safely Communicate: - emails are not shared with other users - members may block each other at any time
            • Send Message
              In order to safely communicate with other members A member May send a written message to one or more members from their "Messages" portal - All messages trigger an on-site notification - Emailed messages should protect the sender's email, but allow reply via email
            • Recieve Message
              In order to safely communicate with other members A member May receive a written message from another member - All messages trigger an on-site notification - The conversation will be visible in their "Messages" portal - Emailed messages should protect the sender's email, but allow reply via email
            • Archive Thread
              In order to manage their read messages A member May archive a message - removed from the inbox, but still searchable - a new message will 'wake' the thread
          • Follow Member
            In order to find mentors and community A member May follow another member to see public posts, questions, studies, etc. - Twitter style, one way 'friending' - No approval required
          • Block Member
            In order to protect members from abuse (spam, hate, bullies) A member May block another member (blocks all messages, posts, etc.) - The blocked member cannot tell they have been blocked - Blocked content is removed from the user's experience
        • Email Verification
          In order to protect the community from automated abuse (spam, hate) The system Will require a member to verify their email before performing certain actions
          • Creating a group
            In order to protect the Group's system from automated members (spammers) The system Will require a member to verify their email before creating a group
          • Message another member
            In order to protect members from automated messaging (spam) The system Will require a member to verify their email before first messaging another user
          • Posting more than 5 times
            In order to protect the community from automated posting (spam) The system Will require a member to verify their email before their 6th post - this allows members to use the site for a reasonable time before checking their email
      • Moderation
        In order to protect members from inaccurate, irrelevant, or abusive content The community Relies on a moderation system with the following design goals: - Respect individual preferences and allow users to tailor their experience Example: Members may block any person or content - removing it for them - Leverage the 'wisdom of crowds' by acting on the community's feedback Example: New posts are automatically pulled for review when the initial response is predominately negative - Provide leaders and staff with tools to prevent abuse & guide conversations Example: A moderation feed allows staff to answer questions or suspend members without leaving the tool
        • Automatically Block Suspicious Posts
          In order to prevent the propagation of malicious content (spam, hate, bully) The system Will flag suspicious posts for moderator approval & will not publish the content Auto blocked content is opaque to the user: - Posts appear posted in the author's UI - The backend responds identically to the http post However, suspicious posts are: - not published to the site - flagged for approval in the moderator's feed What is Questionable content: - believed profane (profanity blacklist) - containing marketing links (domain blacklist) - containing sql injection or script tags - part of a publishing flood - too many posts too quickly - similar or identical content - no other interaction
        • Membership
          In order to address abusive members (violate terms of use - often a spammer) A staff member May use the moderation tools to identify, warn, and suspend
          • Suspicous Member Identification
            In order to suspend abusive members (spam, bullies, hate) A staff member Will have suspicious members highlighted in their moderation feed
            • Account Creation Flood
              In order to prevent spammers from abusing the network The system Should notify moderators when a bunch of similar users create accounts (accounts remain active, but are reviewed by staff) Similar is defined as: - same IP address - same non-standard domain (Not: gmail, yahoo, etc.)
            • Frequently Blocked
              In order to leverage the community's feedback A frequently blocked member Will be added to the staff's moderation feed for review
          • Warn Member
            In order to warn a member who may have unknowingly violated the terms of use A staff member May send the member a suspension warning (working from a form letter) - the event is recorded and presented to the staff member in future interactions - the email goes to the user regardless of their email preferences
          • Suspend Member
            In order to remove a problematic member (spammers, bullies) A staff member May suspend a member's account. - The member is emailed - You have violated the Terms of Service agreement - Your account has been suspended - A link and explanation about the appeals process - Their content is removed from the site - The member's account is frozen (they are logged out permanently)
          • Domain Suspension
            In order to block an automated spam campaign A staff member (Extreme discretion required) May block all membership requests (and auto suspend members) from a single domain Example: <anything>@sell1134.com - these membership requests should be directed to Suspension Appeal - existing members should be auto suspended (including the email)
        • Moderation Feed
          In order to effectively moderate the community A leader or staff member Will receive an activity feed of significant or questionable content. The feed allows the user to do the following w/out leaving the tool: - block a member - remove abusive posts - comment or answer question
          • Leader
            I order to escalate important group activity to a leader's attention The system Aggregates significant and questionable group activity into a moderation feed
          • Staff
            I order to escalate potentially abusive or incorrect content The system Aggregates significant and questionable activity into a staff moderation feed
        • Reaction Profiles
          In order to protect the community from abusive content The system Automatically targets content for moderation using a series of profiles Reaction Types: - Very Positive: member follow - Positive: star - Negative: post block - Very Negative: user block
          • Fire
            In order to triage clearly abusive content The system will remove content for moderation (removed so it can be reviewed) When almost all reactions to a post are negative (low threshold w/ short time ) Thresholds: - At least 5 negative or 3 very negative reactions - Less than 2 (very) positive reactions - Reactions occur over a 2 day period (See parent for reaction definitions)
          • Consistent Opposition
            In order to remove clearly abusive content The system will remove content for moderation (removed so it can be reviewed) When almost all reactions to a post are negative (high threshold w/ no time limit) Thresholds: - At least 12 negative or 8 very negative reactions - More than 70% Opposition - Reactions occur over any time period
          • Controversial
            In order to bring wisdom to conflicted subjects The system will escalate topics to group leaders (or all leaders for public posts) When the post receives a large mix of reactions in a short period of time
          • Blocked by Leadership
            In order to give weight and accountability to a group's leadership When a leader blocks content in their group Content is removed globally AND intermittently sent to staff-moderation (to protect against abuse of leadership situations)
          • Blocked by Staff
            In order to give weight and accountability to staff When a staff member blocks content Content is removed globally AND intermittently sent to another member for staff-moderation
